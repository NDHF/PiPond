So what is this, anyway?

The Pi Pond calculates Pi by firing cannonballs at random into a pond.

The simulation plots random coordinates onto a square that contains a circle of identical width.

Pi is the number of coordinates that fell inside the circle, divided by the total number of coordinates plotted, multiplied by four.

The Pi Pond works due to the relationship between the area of a square and the area of a circle.

It was inspired by a challenge in A.K. Dewdney's The Armchair Universe, a collection of articles about computers from Scientific American.

The Pi Pond was programmed using Vanilla JS and the HTML5 Canvas element.
